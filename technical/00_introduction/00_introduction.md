# Chapter 0: Introduction

AI coding tools are evolving fast. Autocomplete, chat assistants, agentic copilots, autonomous agents — new capabilities ship every month. But most developers are only scratching the surface. Whether you've never tried an AI coding tool, you're already using Cursor or Copilot daily, or you've experimented with Claude Code or Devin — there's a good chance you're leaving a lot on the table.

This guide takes you from the fundamentals to building your own agents and workflows.

## Why This Guide

There's no shortage of AI coding tools. What's missing is a structured path from "what is this?" to "I'm shipping features with agents." Most resources are either surface-level tutorials or deep technical papers. This guide fills the gap — practical, example-driven, and designed to get you productive.

You'll learn how these tools work, how to communicate with them effectively, how to prepare your projects for AI-assisted development, and how to build your own reusable prompts and agents. Each chapter builds on the previous one, but you can jump to any section that matches where you are right now.

## Who This Is For

- **Developers new to AI coding tools** who want a structured starting point — not a YouTube rabbit hole
- **Developers already using Copilot or Cursor** who want to go deeper into agentic workflows, custom skills, and autonomous agents
- **Teams preparing to adopt AI-assisted development** and looking for training material they can work through together
- No prior AI experience required. Just bring your editor and curiosity.

## How to Use This Guide

- **Self-study** — read the chapters in order, try the exercises at the end of each one
- **Team workshop** — present one or two chapters per session; the guide is designed to work as a 2-day training
- **Reference** — jump to any chapter; each one is self-contained
